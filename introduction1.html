Summer Project
Climate Change, Public Health and Baltimore

Top photo by Maris Medina, Capital News Service


A hot summer day in Baltimore can be deadly. Climate change has driven a steady increase in global average temperatures over the last century — and will continue to do so into the future.


In fact, multiday stretches of extreme heat in places including Baltimore could become more common because of climate change’s effect on weather patterns.

This report, “Summer Project,” focuses on warm weather. Capital News Service — with partners including NPR and Wide Angle Youth Media — will spend the summer reporting on how the region’s notorious heat and humidity affects the health of Baltimore residents.
