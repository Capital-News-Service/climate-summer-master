HOW COLD WEATHER AFFECTS HEALTH

Climate change is bringing hotter summers and an increase in global average temperatures. But deep winter cold snaps are unlikely to vanish in Baltimore -- and could, in fact, become more common. That will mean continued problems for people with chronic medical conditions affected by cold weather.

Next

Respiratory Disease

  • Who's most at risk: children and seniors.

  • Conditions: Asthma, COPD, bronchitis.

  • Winter problems: Cold, dry air can trigger respiratory attacks.

Next

Heart Disease

  • Who's most at risk: Seniors.

  • Conditions: Heart-related chest pain, congestive artery disease, heart attacks.

  • Winter problems: Veins and arteries constrict at lower temperatures, stressing the cardiovascular system.

Next

Hypothermia

  • Who's most at risk: People who lack shelter.

  • Winter problems: With prolonged exposure to cold, body temperature drops dangerously low, affecting the heart, lungs and other organ systems.
