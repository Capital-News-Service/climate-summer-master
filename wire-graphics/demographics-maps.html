High temps correlate with high poverty, lower life expectancy and more.

Temperatures across Baltimore can vary up to 10 degrees on a hot summer afternoon, according to a federally-funded study from 2018. This "urban heat island effect" is illustrated by neighborhood in the large map below. An analysis of neighborhood demographics found a correlation between the temperature of a neighborhood and certain quality-of-life factors. Neighborhoods with a higher ambient temperature tend to have more families living below the poverty live, lower life expectancy, higher violent crime rates and a higher rate of employment then lower-temperature neighborhoods, as seen in the smaller maps below.
